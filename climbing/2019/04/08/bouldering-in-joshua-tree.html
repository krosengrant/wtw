Bouldering in Joshua Tree

April 08, 2019

This past weekend a group of friends and I decided to brave the government shut down and visit Joshua Tree. We weren’t sure what to expect and were prepared for crowds, trash, and camping on BLM (land outside of the park where camping is permitted). None of these stressors were encountered. Outside of campsites there were few people, the trash and toilets were well taken care of, and we were even lucky enough to stumble across an open campsite at Ryan Campground.

We spent the weekend hiking to boulders and attempting to climb them. For all but one of us it was our first time climbing outside of a gym and it wasn’t at all what we expected. Despite the problems being of a much lower height we were intimidated by the rough texture of the rocks and the seemingly high consequences of falling against a substrate that felt like a cheese grater. Not only were the holds smaller but the footholds were often just little nubs or cracks along the rock surface. We had been majorly babied by the gyms.

Halfway through the trip we learned that an outdoor V0 was equivalent to an indoor V3 or V4… Oops. With that in mind we began tackling sub V0’s to build up our confidence and by the end of the trip approached the V0’s with more knowledge and confidence. Though we were largely defeated by many routes we got a taste of outdoor climbing and will certainly be back for more.
