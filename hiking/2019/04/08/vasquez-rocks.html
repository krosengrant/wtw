Vasquez Rocks

April 08, 2019

An ethereal landscape fitting of another planet Vasquez Rocks is only an hour outside of LA but seems like a different world. The rocks emerge from the ground at unnatural angles, like books leaning in a row, creating complex rock formations that are quite easy for anyone to ascend. It makes the perfect day trip out of LA, and is relatively empty, at least during winter. Katie and I visited Vasquez rocks just before Christmas immediately after a rainstorm and were able to witness dramatic clouds pushed around by chilling winds as we scrambled up the rocky terrain.

Compared to other places with climbable rocks, Vasquez Rocks is not only easier to climb/hike, but are also finer grain, meaning that, worst case scenario, one can climb the most recognizable rock formation barefoot, as Katie did, which she highly recommends. Otherwise, it is a prudent idea to bring shoes with a good grip to aid in ascending steep inclines.

The great thing about Vasquez Rocks is it is accessible, even to the most budget conscious college students, as the park itself has no entry fee yet has similar facilities to that of a National Park. The visitors center’s walls are lined with maps and small glass enclosures with a small variety of local animals. Inside, park rangers are available for any questions or trail recommendations.

In addition to the wide variety of hiking trails, a portion of the PCT also runs through the park, providing the opportunity to walk just a tiny portion of the 2,650 mile trail. Of the hiking trails, there are trails for both pedestrians and equestrians. There is also the less expensive and legal option of participating in day horseback riding programs in the area.

The unique landscape not only allows for a fun hiking experience, but also dramatic photographs (according to Katie, all of my photographs were subpar). This unique landscape has in turn made Vasquez rocks a prominent filming location where notable TV Shows and Movies, such as Star Trek and Austin Powers, have been filmed.
