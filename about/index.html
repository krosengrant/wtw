Katie

University of California, Santa Cruz
Computer Science

The tranquility of hiking and backpacking has always appealed to me more so than any other type of travel. The first time I went backpacking was, as cliche as it sounds, a life changing experience. I was in a bit of a dark place in high school and our trips gave me something to look forward to. There is something about the solitude and simplicity of it all. Carrying everything you need on your back, hiking miles away from any communication with the outside world. If you become injured, encounter a wild animal, or (hypothetically) forgot the water filter, you and your fellow backpackers are left to your own devices. As terrifying as this may sound, it somehow brings comfort to me, the knowledge that you only have your wits to rely on. Time after time, I find myself hiking up hills in the hot summer sun for mile after mile, all the while wondering why I am putting myself through this. Every time I go backpacking, I face this feeling and yet, there is nothing as rewarding as seeing the campsite for the night after that last grueling hill. The finality of knowing you have made it and have no more miles to travel, the chance to put down your pack and just relax.
